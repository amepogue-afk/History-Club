Degree Pathways

Every path through history, in one department.

From an undergraduate concentration to a joint PhD with UC Santa Barbara, the History Department at Sac State offers more pathways than most students realize.

Undergraduate

History BA

The core undergraduate degree with flexibility to focus on U.S., World, or thematic history. Includes lower-division survey courses and upper-division seminars where you do the kind of research and writing that actually prepares you for what comes next.

Concentration

History BA, Law and Social Justice

Pairs American constitutional, legal, and political history with the global and U.S. social movements shaping civil and human rights. Built specifically for students considering law school, policy careers, politics, and social justice work. Coordinated by Dr. Mona Siegel.

The Clio Journal

Your research could win a national prize. Ours already has.

Clio is our student-run, student-edited history journal published every spring for nearly three decades. It features original research from undergraduate and graduate students across history, public history, and related fields.

The journal has won national recognition in the Gerald D. Nash History Journal Prize competition of Phi Alpha Theta more times than any comparable student publication in the CSU system.

A publication credit in Clio belongs on your graduate school application, your law school application, and your resume. Editing it gives you hands-on experience in the full publishing process, from peer review to final layout. Either way, it is something concrete to point to when you are competing for what comes next.

Concentration Spotlight

History, Law, and Social Justice

Law school wants students who can read complicated material, build arguments from evidence, and write under pressure. That is exactly what this concentration trains you to do. You study American constitutional and legal history alongside the social movements that shaped civil and human rights law, which means you arrive at law school knowing not just how the system works but why it was built the way it was.

The concentration is coordinated by Dr. Mona Siegel, a National Endowment for the Humanities Fellow and national award-winning scholar whose research focuses on peace, education, and social reform. She knows this terrain from the inside and mentors students through every step of the pathway.

Pre-law advising is built into the program. You will have access to dedicated advisors, a clear course roadmap, and a faculty coordinator who can speak specifically to your law school or career goals. If you want to do work that matters, this is where you build the foundation for it.

For Transfer Students

You already belong here.

Transfer students make up a significant part of this department and the community is built to welcome you. You are not arriving late. You are arriving with more context, more life experience, and more intentionality than most first-year students bring.

The History Department has clear degree roadmaps for every pathway, accessible advising, and a student community that is collaborative rather than competitive. You can get involved in the History Club, Phi Alpha Theta, and the Clio journal from day one.

Email the Department

You know exactly where you stand

Every pathway has a published degree roadmap. You can see exactly which courses you need, which ones may transfer, and how long it realistically takes. No hunting for answers.

Advisors who actually know you

Every concentration has a dedicated faculty coordinator. Dr. Chloe Burke runs pre-credential advising. Dr. Mona Siegel coordinates Law and Social Justice. These are real people with real office hours who respond to email.

A community worth joining

The History Club, Phi Alpha Theta, and the Clio journal are open from day one. You do not have to wait until you have been here a year to start building the connections that matter.

Scholarship opportunities

The College of Arts and Letters has scholarship support for history students at every stage. Reach out to the department early and ask what is available for your situation.

The Location Advantage

Your classroom is the state capital.

Sacramento is not just a backdrop. It is an active advantage. You are surrounded by the California State Archives, the state legislature, federal courts, major nonprofit organizations, government agencies, and one of the highest concentrations of history institutions in California.

For public history students this is a live, functioning laboratory that most programs cannot replicate. For pre-law students, you are watching legislation get made in real time. For anyone interested in government, policy, or advocacy work, you are already there.

The Public History program has built community partnerships across the Sacramento region, connecting students directly with internship sites, professional networks, and career placements.

Get Involved

History here is not just coursework.

The History Club is open to everyone: history majors and non-majors, undergrads and grad students. We organize things worth showing up for.

🥾

Guided Hikes and Field Trips

Museum and archive tours, guided hikes to historical sites, and picnics. History does not have to mean sitting in a library. We take it outside whenever we can.

🎙️

Networking with Professionals

We host events connecting students directly with historians, archivists, lawyers, government officials, and other professionals who actually used their history degrees to build careers.

🛠️

Career Workshops

Practical sessions on grad school applications, law school prep, resume writing for history graduates, and how to talk about what your degree actually taught you in an interview.

📰

The Clio Journal

Publish your own research or join the editorial team. The Clio is nationally award-winning, entirely student-run, and one of the most distinctive things you can put on any application.

🏅

Phi Alpha Theta

Our Rho Xi chapter of the national history honor society. Academic workshops, the PAT regional conference each spring, and a professional network that extends far beyond Sacramento.

🎧

Out of the Dust Podcast

A podcast produced by graduate students in the Public History program. Real historical research, made accessible to broader audiences. You could be part of a future season.
